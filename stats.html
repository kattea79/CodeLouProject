Stats

Throughout the battle statisticians have been watching and calculating. 'What have they learned?' you ask...

We should begin by creating a better understanding of items tracked:

Fur Piles

Fur plies exponmentially grow and beget new fur piles. Once they have grown large enough and dense enough they have the potential of coming to life to create a new cat. Hence the common measurement is called a furcat.

The creation of furpiles can be organic (through cats laying/grooming in the same location), through Bystander grooming (with a brush, etc) or through battle. When it happens through battle the growth is quick and looks painful. An expectation of a partially bald cat roaming around the house often arises after finding one of these furpiles.

Bystander Wounds

As with any war civilian casualties occasionally occur. With cat wars the Bystanders often wonder if they are unintentional victims or if the cats have malicious intent. We will not be addressing these philosophical questions.

Some of the most common Bystander wounds include:

  1. Trampoline - when a cat jumps from another surface on to you and immediately launches off of you at another cat.
  2. Lying in Wait - when a cat is seemingly asleep on your lap and out of nowhere launches at another cat who just walked in the room.
  3. Hit and Run - when a cat is so focused on attacking her enemy that she goes barreling over your bare foot tearing it up with her claws.
